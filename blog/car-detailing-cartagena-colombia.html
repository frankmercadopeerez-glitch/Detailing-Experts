Why Cartagena is Hard on Your Car

Cartagena sits on the Caribbean coast of Colombia — which means your car faces a combination that's rare in most cities:

  • Salt air: Marine particles deposit on your paint, chassis and mechanical components daily, accelerating corrosion 3-5x faster than inland cities.
  • Heat: Temperatures of 32-38°C year-round degrade plastics, leather and rubber faster.
  • Humidity: 80%+ relative humidity combined with salt = perfect conditions for rust.
  • UV radiation: Among the highest in Colombia — fades paint and damages interior quickly.

SiO₂ liquid glass coating that creates a hydrophobic barrier over the paint. Repels water, salt, UV rays and contaminants for 2-5 years. Keeps your car looking freshly washed and significantly reduces maintenance effort in Cartagena's climate.
